Chamaleons

  1. Chameleons change their colour due to a number of different factors, including their mood, changes in light or temperature, or the humidity of their environment.
  2. They can move their eyes separately, with each eye having a field of 180 degrees. This means they can see 360 degrees if they need to!
  3. 59 unique species of chameleon live on the island of Madagascar and are found nowhere else. This is over a quarter of the world’s 202 species.

Bears

  1. Bears care deeply about family members.
  2. Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio; and use tools in various contexts.
  3. Some species of Asiatic bear build nests in the trees. They can use these for hiding, eating and even sleeping.

Pandas

  1. Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  2. Although pandas are 99% vegetarian their digestive system is typical of a carnivore. For the 1% of their diet that isn't bamboo, pandas eat eggs, small animals, carrion, and forage in farmland for pumpkin, kidney beans, wheat and domestic pig food.
  3. Sometimes, to mark their scent, panda's climb a tree backwards with their hindfeet until they're in a full handstand upside down - enabling them to leave their scent higher up.

Camels

  1. These desert animals can survive for 6-7 months without water. Nonetheless, whenever they access to water they can drink as much as 32 gallons in one drinking session!
  2. Camels can run as fast as 65 kilometers per hour and sustain a walking speed of 40 kilometers per hour.
  3. Camels have a thick layer of skin which makes it possible for them to sit on the hot sand without being burned.
